Next steps for your planning application

Decide what you need for your planning application

Contact your local planning authority to check their requirements.


Your local planning authority is Bristol City Council.


Get a flood risk assessment (FRA). You will normally need to pay a flood risk specialist to carry it out.


For developments in flood zone 3 your FRA should include an assessment of all sources of flooding and take into account climate change allowances.


Find out how to do an assessment.


Find out more about flood zones and what they mean.


When using the surface water map on the check your long term flood risk service the following considerations apply:


  • surface water extents are suitable for use in planning
  • surface water climate change scenarios may help to inform risk assessments, but the available data fall short of what is required to assess planned development
  • surface water depth information should not be used for planning purposes

To find out about other factors that might affect the flood risk of this location, you should also check: 


  • reservoir flood risk 
  • groundwater flood risk – you could use the British Geological Survey groundwater flooding data, groundwater: current status and flood risk and the guide on mining and groundwater constraints for development – further information may be available from the lead local flood authority (LLFA)
  • your local planning authority’s strategic flood risk assessment (SFRA), which includes future flood risk.

Other data you may need

You will also need to assess residual risk from flood defence breach in this location. If available, we will provide breach hazard mapping data to help you do this.


Get help

For help deciding what flood risk information you need, email the Environment Agency team in Greater Manchester, Merseyside and Cheshire at Inforequests.GMMC@environment-agency.gov.uk.
