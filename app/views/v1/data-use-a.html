How you can use the data

Data suitable for planning activities

Flood risk assessment

You can use all the data layers to inform a Flood risk assessment.

To identify the need for Flood Risk Assessment use:

  • Flood zones 2 and 3
  • Water storage areas
  • All rivers and sea, and surface water layers

Exception and sequential test

To inform application of exception and sequential test use:

  • Flood zones 2 and 3
  • Defences
  • All rivers and sea, and surface water layers
  • Water storage areas**

To identify the need for exception test use:

  • Flood zones 2 and 3
  • water storage areas**
  • Rivers and sea 3.3%**

To identify the need for sequential test use:

  • Flood zones 2 an 3
  • Water storage areas**
  • All rivers and sea, and surface water layes

Local planning authority designation of 3b (functional floodplain) in strategic flood risk assessment

To Inform local planning authority designation of 3b (functional floodplain) in strategic flood risk assessment use:

  • water storage areas***
  • Rivers and sea 3.3%***

Development incompatible Table 2 PPG

To check development incompatible – Table 2 PPG use:

  • Flood zones 2 and 3
  • Water storage areas***
  • Rivers and sea 3.3%***

Local planning authority to consult Environment Agency or apply standing advice

To trigger for local planning authority to consult Environment Agency or apply standing advice use:

  • Flood zones*
  • Main rivers*
  • Water storage areas**
  • Rivers and sea 3.3%**
  • All Climate change Rivers and sea layers****

* subject to apply the process set out in National flood Risk Standing Advice for local planning authorities

** If local planning authority’s strategic flood risk assessment defines as Flood Zone 3b

*** subject to local agreement with the Environment Agency

**** Only if site location is FZ1, in which case, apply National flood risk standing advice for local planning authorities as if site were Flood Zone 2
